Workshops & Programs

Non-Certificate programs for all ages

  • Rock School
  • Family Lessons
  • Choir
  • Aerophone Go Breathing Project

Blackpool Music School offers group music sessions of an hour to socialise and develop musical skills. The Rock Star project aims to ignite young teenagers’ interest in music. Students can choose from keyboard, drums, guitar, or vocals. The project runs on Saturdays for about an hour and is open to all teenagers.

This fun event culminates in a live music performance showcasing the students’ skills. Teenagers of all abilities are welcome, regardless of prior musical experience. 

Family lessons offer a unique and enjoyable way for families to bond over music. These group lessons cater to families of up to five members, providing a supportive and interactive learning environment. Participants can choose from a variety of instruments, such as piano, guitar, or drums, and learn together at their own pace.

The lessons are designed to be fun and engaging, encouraging family members to collaborate and create music as a team. Whether you're looking to start a new hobby, improve your skills, or simply spend quality time together, Blackpool Music School's family lessons provide a memorable and enriching experience for all ages.

Blackpool Music School's choir offers a vibrant and inclusive community for music enthusiasts of all ages. Our choir members come together to share their passion for singing, learn new repertoire, and perform in a variety of settings. Whether you're a seasoned singer or just starting out, our choir provides a welcoming and supportive atmosphere where you can develop your vocal skills and build lasting friendships.

Maxine Bradley, a 78-year-old asthma sufferer, found relief after learning to play the clarinet with the help of Blackpool Music School. The school’s founder plans to conduct research on the health benefits of wind instruments, using the Aerophone Go, an electric saxophone that can help strengthen lungs.

“My breathing is much better, even for an amateur, and as far as the trials are concerned, I’m all for that. It’s really helped me and if it helps other people then that is great.” - Maxine Bradley
